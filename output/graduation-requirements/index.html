Graduation Requirements

Criteria that students must meet to successfully complete their educational program and graduate.

Description

Graduation requirements refer to the set of standards and criteria established by educational institutions that students must fulfill to be eligible for graduation. These requirements often encompass a combination of credit hours, specific courses, assessments, and sometimes other activities such as community service or internships. For high school students, graduation requirements typically include completing a certain number of core subjects like mathematics, science, and English, along with elective courses. In higher education, graduation requirements may involve completing a major, a minor, general education courses, and maintaining a minimum GPA. Additionally, some programs may require students to pass comprehensive exams, complete a thesis, or participate in practical experiences. Meeting these requirements ensures that students have attained the necessary knowledge and skills for their chosen field and are prepared for further education or the workforce.

Examples

  • High school diploma requirements may include 4 years of English, 3 years of math, and 2 years of science.
  • A college degree may require a minimum of 120 credit hours, including core courses and electives.

Additional Information

  • Requirements can vary significantly between different states and educational institutions.
  • Students should consult their school's academic advisor to understand specific graduation requirements.
